Multi-Modal Imaging of the Aging Brain

Broadly, I am interested in using magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) and positron emission tomography (PET) to understand brain changes that are associated with aging and with neurodegenerative disorders.

My first taste of science was in the Jagust Lab at UC Berkeley. There, I worked on projects that used fMRI and amyloid-PET to investigate the relationship between healthy brain network connectivity and the distribution of amyloid plaques in aging and in preclinical Alzheimer's disease.

Nowadays, I do research in the Brewer Lab at UCSD. My current project is using using longitudinal MRI, amyloid-PET, and tau-PET to assess the relationships between amyloid, tau, and neurodegeneration in the earliest stages of Alzheimer's disease.

Open-Source Software

My weapon of choice for my work is Python and I am a frequent user of modules such as NumPy, SciPy, and NiBabel. My research has benefited greatly from freely available, open-source software so I think it's only right that I make an effort to contribute to a community that has given me so much. I am making a conscious effort to post my code online in case bits and pieces of it are helpful to others.

My first full-scale project will be pet2mri, a set of tools for processing, quantifying, and organizing PET data. It will essentially be a set of scripts that are wrappers for various FreeSurfer commands. It's not available on pip yet, but a bare-bones version can be downloaded. Please see the documentation for more information.
